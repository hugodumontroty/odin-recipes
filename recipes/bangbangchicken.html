Bang Bang Chicken

Description

This vibrant bang bang chicken recipe is full of bold flavours, beautifully balancing chilli, peanut and lime. A tasty and healthy dish you can make using leftover roast chicken which guarantees to put a smile on your face.

Ingredients

  • 4 tbsp peanut butter
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 lime, juiced
  • 1/2 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1/2 tbsp sunflower oil
  • 4 chicken thigh fillets, thinly sliced
  • 1 red chilli, sliced
  • 75g soya beans
  • 1 carrot, finely sliced into ribbons
  • 200g pak choi, washed and sliced
  • 200g tenderstem broccoli
  • 200g shiitake mushrooms, thinly sliced
  • 1 spring onions, sliced on the diagonal
  • 300g ready-cooked rice noodles

Steps

  1. Boil the soya beans in water for 2 minutes, drain, refresh in cold water and set aside.
  2. Mix 1 tbsp water into the peanut butter, then stir in the soy sauce, lime juice and sesame oil. Set aside.
  3. Heat the sunflower oil in a wok over a high heat until smoking, then add the chicken and stir-fry for 3 minutes until browned. Toss in the chilli, carrot, pak choi, broccoli and mushrooms, then cook for a further 3 minutes.
  4. Add the rice noodles and soya beans to the wok and stir-fry for 1 minute, then gently fold in the peanut sauce until everything is warmed through. Serve with the spring onions and extra soy sauce, if liked.
